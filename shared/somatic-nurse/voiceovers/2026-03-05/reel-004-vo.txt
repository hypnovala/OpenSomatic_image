Voiceover (20–35 seconds)

Delivery notes:
• calm tone
• slow pacing
• breath pauses

Script:
If your shift is over but your body still feels braced—jaw tight, shoulders up—
that’s not you being “bad at relaxing.”

That’s protection.

In a hospital, your nervous system learns: stay ready.
Alarms, urgency, unpredictability—your brain keeps predicting more demand.
So your muscles hold a ready position.

And even when you clock out, your stress chemistry can take time to come down.

Try a 30-second unbrace right now:
Long exhale…
soften your jaw and tongue…
drop your shoulders…
and open your hands.

Do it at transitions—car, doorway, shower—so your body learns: it’s safe to stand down.

Education only, not medical advice.
