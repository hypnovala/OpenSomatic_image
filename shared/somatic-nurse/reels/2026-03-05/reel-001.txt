Title
Nervous System Reset After a Hospital Shift (5-Minute Ritual)

Hook (<=12 words)
Still wired after shift? Reset before you carry it home.

Reel beats (5–7)
1) Cold open: you in scrubs, keys in hand, eyes tired—“If your body feels on-call…”
2) Name the pattern: adrenaline + responsibility can keep your nervous system in “go” even when the shift ends.
3) 30-second orienting: look left/right, notice 5 neutral objects; let your exhale lengthen.
4) Physiological sigh x2: inhale through nose, top-up sip inhale, long slow exhale; shoulders drop.
5) Discharge the day: gentle shake-out (hands, arms, legs) for 20 seconds; then stillness.
6) Boundary cue: wash hands slowly or change shoes while saying: “Shift is over. I’m off-duty.”
7) Close: “Try this before scrolling—tell me what changes in your chest/jaw.”

On-screen text (3 lines)
1) Your shift ended.
2) Your nervous system might not know yet.
3) Try this 60-second reset.

Caption (100–160 words)
If you leave the hospital but your body still feels like it’s bracing, you’re not broken—you’re patterned. High-responsibility environments can keep your nervous system in mobilization (wired, restless, hyper-alert) even when you’re finally off the clock. Try this quick reset in your car or at the door: orient to your space (slow eyes left-right), do two physiological sighs, then shake out your hands/arms/legs for 20 seconds and pause. Finish with a simple boundary ritual—wash your hands slowly or change shoes—while naming the transition: “Shift is over. I’m off-duty.” Small cues teach your body what your calendar already knows.

Education only, not medical advice.
