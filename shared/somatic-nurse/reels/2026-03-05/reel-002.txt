Title
Nervous System Reset After a Hospital Shift (7-Minute Downshift)

Hook (<=12 words)
Still vibrating after shift? Try this 7-minute reset.

Reel Beats (5–7)
1) Relate: “You’re home, but your body’s still at work.”
2) Name it: sympathetic activation after high-alert environments.
3) Step 1 (60 sec): “Doorway drop” — pause at the threshold, exhale longer than inhale x3.
4) Step 2 (2 min): Legs up the wall (or on couch) + hand on belly, feel the exhale soften ribs.
5) Step 3 (2 min): Vagus hum — gentle humming on the exhale (mmm) x5 breaths.
6) Step 4 (2 min): Orienting — slowly look for 5 neutral objects; let your eyes land and soften.
7) Close: pick ONE “done” cue (shower, tea, change clothes) to signal safety.

On-Screen Text (3 lines)
1) “Your shift ended. Your nervous system may not have.”
2) “7 minutes to downshift (no fancy tools).”
3) “Exhale • Legs up • Hum • Orient”

Caption (100–160 words)
After a hospital shift, your nervous system can stay in “go-mode” even when you’re finally home. That’s not weakness—it’s physiology. You spent hours tracking alarms, needs, and risk. Of course your body is still scanning.

Try this 7-minute reset to help your system register safety:
1) Pause at the doorway and do 3 long exhales.
2) Put your legs up the wall (or on the couch) and breathe into your belly.
3) Hum softly on the exhale to encourage a downshift.
4) Slowly orient: let your eyes land on 5 neutral objects.

Pick one “done” cue (shower, tea, changing clothes) to close the loop.

Education only, not medical advice.
