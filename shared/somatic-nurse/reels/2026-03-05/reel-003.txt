Title
Post-Shift Nervous System Reset for Nurses (3-Minute Parking Lot Protocol)

Hook (<=12 words)
Do this before you drive home—shift ends in your body.

Reel Beats (5–7)
1) Hook/relate: “You clocked out, but your body didn’t.”
2) Reframe: this is your nervous system finishing the stress cycle.
3) Step 1 (45 sec): “Physiological sigh” x3 (two short inhales, long exhale).
4) Step 2 (60 sec): Unclench scan—jaw, tongue, shoulders, hands; soften each on the exhale.
5) Step 3 (45 sec): Butterfly taps (cross arms, gentle alternating taps) + slow exhale.
6) Step 4 (30 sec): Orienting—name 3 colors, 2 shapes, 1 sound.
7) Close: “Now drive home with a different baseline.”

On-Screen Text (3 lines)
1) “For nurses: a 3-minute post-shift reset”
2) “Sigh • Unclench • Tap • Orient”
3) “You don’t have to carry shift home.”

Caption (100–160 words)
Nurses: if you get to your car and realize you’re holding your breath, clenching your jaw, or replaying the shift—your nervous system is doing its job. It doesn’t instantly switch from high-alert to safe.

Try this 3-minute “parking lot protocol” before you drive:
• 3 physiological sighs (two quick inhales, one long exhale)
• Unclench scan: jaw, tongue, shoulders, hands—soften each on the exhale
• Butterfly taps: cross your arms and alternate gentle taps while you breathe slowly
• Orient: name 3 colors, 2 shapes, and 1 sound

This isn’t about “calming down.” It’s about giving your body a clear signal: the emergency is over.

Education only, not medical advice.
